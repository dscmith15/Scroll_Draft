                                                              Businesses today often keep track of large amounts of both financial and non-financial information. Sales people keep track of current and potential customers; marketing people keep track of product details and regional demographics; accounting people keep track of financial data and issue reports. To be useful, all this data must be organized into a useful system. Such a system is called a management information system. The financial hub of this system is accounting. Accounting is the information system that records, analyzes, and reports financial information, which lets people make better choices when deciding what to do with economic resources. It is a tool that lets the user, whether a business or an individual, to make wiser, more informed economic choices. A financial accounting system is intended for use by both the management of an organization and those outside of it. Because it is important that financial accounting reports be understood correctly, financial accounting is subject to a set of specific guidelines.                                                              .
